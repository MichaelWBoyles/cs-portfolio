### Essential Questions

**How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?**  
I interpret user needs by translating requests into user stories with clear acceptance criteria, then implementing and testing until the result matches what “done” means. User stories keep the work focused on user value instead of just technical tasks. Acceptance criteria also reduces confusion and rework, especially when requirements change.

**How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?**  
I approach development iteratively by delivering small increments, testing frequently, and using feedback to adjust priorities. The Agile practices I plan to keep using are sprint planning, short daily check-ins for progress and blockers, sprint review to confirm “done,” and retrospectives to improve how I work. I also want to keep using a visible backlog to maintain transparency.

**What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?**  
Being a good team member means communicating clearly, keeping work transparent, and supporting the team goal over individual preferences. It also means asking questions early when requirements are unclear, being reliable with commitments, and protecting quality through testing and review. Good teamwork includes adapting to change without blame and helping the team improve over time.
